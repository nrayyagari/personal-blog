# Introduction

UPSC CSE consists of 3 stages. 

Preliminary - 2 papers, objective format, done in 1 day 
Mains - 9 papers, written format(physical writing), done in 5 days
Interview - 30-45 min Interactive session with a panel

Now this series of posts concerns the Preliminary part. Let me understand importance of Prelims


1. It’s the first stage of the exam cycle. You don’t clear Prelims, you can’t progress to mains stages. As simple as that 
2. There are several teachers/coaching institutes to guide your mains exams. But for Prelims, I genuinely believe, aspirant has to make the most effort. It can be whether in terms of attempting paper or practise. 
3. There are lot of available material/sources in market. This is where difficulty is. Separating wheat from the chaff. 

# What makes UPSC Prelims a thing to ponder?

It’s supposed to be a preliminary test right. An entry level thing before hitting the gigantic mains. So what’s so much fuss about it?  

This may be notion among aspirants before starting their UPSC journey. But let me tell you some interesting things about Prelims

1. The commission deliberately inserts randomness into paper every year. I believe this is done so institutes/aspirants don’t identify the patterns to crack it.
2. You see, people sit for exams like JEE, NEET etc. A guy/girl within top 10 in a particular year, can be sure that if they write the exam again next year with adequate preparation, they can score good, even if not in top 10 again. But there is no such thing with UPSC Prelims. Many toppers who have been selected for services also can fail next year. Nothing to get surprised!
3. Anyone who has appeared for Prelims once can say this for sure-Even the next AIR1 does not know whether he gets through the exam, a day before the actual exam. 
4. Ever since, Prelims for Forest services was merged with CSE, proportion of Environment based questions has shot up. So there is more to read now. It can happen similar thing with S&T since railways are also following similar pattern as IFoS.
